📖 How to Play

New to Pokémon TCG? Learn the basics and start your journey as a Trainer!

🎬 Watch Tutorial

Watch this video to get a quick overview of how the game works before reading the steps below.

📌 Click "Mark as Read" on each step to track your progress!

01

🃏 Set Up Your Deck

Each player needs a deck of exactly 60 cards. Your deck can include Pokémon cards, Trainer cards, and Energy cards. Shuffle your deck thoroughly before the game begins.

  • Draw 7 cards as your starting hand.
  • Place 6 Prize Cards face down — you earn one each time you knock out an opponent's Pokémon.
  • Place one Basic Pokémon face down as your Active Pokémon.
  • Place up to 5 Basic Pokémon on your Bench.
02

🔄 Your Turn Structure

Each turn follows a set order. You must complete each phase before moving on to the next.

  • Draw Phase: Draw one card from the top of your deck.
  • Main Phase: Play Trainer cards, attach one Energy card, evolve Pokémon, or place Basic Pokémon on your Bench.
  • Attack Phase: Use your Active Pokémon to attack. Your turn ends after attacking.
03

⚡ Attaching Energy

Pokémon need Energy cards to use their attacks. You may attach one Energy card per turn from your hand to any of your Pokémon.

  • Each attack has an Energy cost shown on the card.
  • Energy types must match unless the attack accepts Colorless Energy.
  • Energy stays on your Pokémon until it is knocked out or removed by a card effect.
04

⚔️ Attacking & Damage

When you attack, apply the damage listed on the attack to your opponent's Active Pokémon. Some attacks also have special effects!

  • Weakness: If the defending Pokémon is weak to your attack type, multiply damage by ×2.
  • Resistance: Some Pokémon resist certain types — subtract 30 from the damage.
  • Place damage counters (10 damage each) on the defending Pokémon.
  • When a Pokémon's HP reaches 0, it is Knocked Out.
05

🏆 Winning the Game

There are three ways to win a game of Pokémon TCG:

  • Collect all 6 Prize Cards by knocking out your opponent's Pokémon.
  • Your opponent has no Pokémon left on the field or Bench.
  • Your opponent cannot draw a card at the start of their turn.

The first player to meet any of these conditions wins the match!

06

🌟 Types of Cards

Understanding the three card types is essential to building a strong deck.

  • Pokémon Cards: The main fighters. Can be Basic, Stage 1, or Stage 2 (evolved forms).
  • Trainer Cards: Items, Supporters, and Stadiums that give powerful one-time or ongoing effects.
  • Energy Cards: Power your Pokémon's attacks. Basic Energy can be included in unlimited quantities in your deck.

📋 Quick Reference

🃏
Deck Size

Exactly 60 cards per deck. Maximum 4 copies of any single card except Basic Energy.

🎖️
Prize Cards

Take 6 Prize Cards at game start. Draw one each time you knock out an opponent's Pokémon.

⚡
Energy Per Turn

Attach only one Energy card per turn. Choose wisely — the right Energy at the right time wins games.
